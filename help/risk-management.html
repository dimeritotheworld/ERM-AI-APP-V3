Risk Scoring Explained

Risk scores help prioritize which risks need the most attention. Dimeri ERM uses a standard 5x5 risk matrix.

Inherent vs. Residual Risk

üî¥ Inherent Risk

The risk level before any controls are applied.

Inherent Risk = Likelihood √ó Impact

Example: Likelihood 4 √ó Impact 5 = Score 20 (Critical)

üü¢ Residual Risk

The risk level after controls are implemented.

Adding Risk Details

Beyond basic information, detailed risk documentation improves understanding and enables better mitigation strategies.

üìù Description

A clear explanation of what the risk is and how it could affect the organization. Include context about why this risk exists.

Example: "The main conveyor belt system is operating beyond its designed lifespan. Failure would halt all production operations and require emergency replacement, leading to significant downtime."

‚ö†Ô∏è Consequences

What happens if the risk materializes? Document both direct and indirect impacts.

Example:
  • Production stoppage (2-4 weeks)
  • Revenue loss of $2M per week
  • Customer delivery delays
  • Potential safety incidents during emergency repairs

üîç Root Causes

The underlying reasons why this risk exists. Understanding causes helps identify appropriate controls.

Example:
  • Equipment exceeds 15-year service life
  • Deferred maintenance due to budget constraints
  • Increased production load beyond original design
  • Limited availability of replacement parts

üë§ Risk Owner

The person accountable for managing this risk. Ownership ensures someone monitors and acts on the risk.

üìÖ Review Date

When this risk should be reassessed. Set regular review cycles based on risk severity and volatility.

Managing Risk Registers

Risk registers are collections of related risks. You can create multiple registers to organize risks by department, project, or any other grouping.

Creating a New Register

1

Access Register Settings

Go to Risk Register and click the register selector dropdown at the top.

2

Create New Register

Click "Create New Register" and enter a name (e.g., "IT Risks", "Project Alpha", "2024 Strategic Risks").

3

Add Risks

Switch to your new register and start adding risks. Each register maintains its own independent list.

Exporting Risk Data

Export your risk register for reporting, backup, or analysis:

  • CSV: Open in Excel for analysis and manipulation
  • JSON: Technical format for data backup or integration
  • PDF: Professional report format for stakeholders

Best Practices

‚úÖ

Review Risks Regularly

Set calendar reminders to review high-priority risks monthly and all risks quarterly. Risk landscapes change‚Äîkeep your register current.

‚úÖ

Assign Clear Owners

Every risk should have a designated owner who is accountable for monitoring and managing that risk. Unowned risks tend to be forgotten.

‚úÖ

Document Root Causes

Understanding why a risk exists helps you address it at the source rather than just treating symptoms. Use the "5 Whys" technique.

‚úÖ

Link Controls to Risks

Always connect controls to the risks they mitigate. This shows residual risk reduction and justifies control investments.

‚úÖ

Use Consistent Scoring

Apply the same criteria when scoring all risks. Consider creating a team scoring guide to ensure consistency across risk owners.

‚úÖ

Involve Stakeholders

Risk identification shouldn't happen in isolation. Involve team members from different areas to get diverse perspectives.

‚ö†Ô∏è
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Creating risks that are too vague or too specific
  • Scoring based on fear rather than evidence
  • Forgetting to update risks when circumstances change
  • Having controls without linked risks (orphaned controls)
  • Not reviewing control effectiveness regularly
